Orbit elements

What is an Orbit?

An orbit is the curved path followed by an object as it moves around another object due to gravity. For example, planets orbit the Sun, moons orbit planets, and artificial satellites orbit Earth.

How Orbits Work

Orbits are created by a balance between two forces: the forward motion of an object and the gravitational pull of the body it is orbiting. Gravity pulls the object inward, while its motion keeps it from falling straight down.

Types of Orbits

  • Circular Orbit: The object moves in a nearly perfect circle.
  • Elliptical Orbit: The most common type; the path is oval-shaped.
  • Parabolic Orbit: The object passes once and escapes.
  • Hyperbolic Orbit: The object moves very fast and leaves the system.
